01.

What is Scaler School of Technology?

Scaler School of Technology is a software-focused undergraduate path based in Bengaluru. The major difference from a traditional BTech route is the curriculum focus. SST is much more centered around software development, computer science fundamentals, and problem solving early on.

For many students, the real comparison is not just "good or bad" but whether they want a broader engineering route, or a more direct software-oriented environment from the start.

  • Software-focused learning environment
  • Bengaluru-based campus with hostel accommodation nearby
  • Peer group that is generally more coding-oriented
  • Alternative route for students comparing JEE, CUET, private entrances, and other college options
02.

Student Experience

Learning environment

Coding-first peer group

From a student perspective, one of the noticeable things about SST is that many students actually spend time on coding, projects, and development. That makes the peer environment feel more aligned with software engineering than a typical first-year structure.

Campus life

Campus and hostel

The campus is smaller than large traditional universities, but the surrounding area is calm and green. The hostel is about 1.5 km away, which makes commuting manageable. Students also get Cult gym passes and there are gyms nearby.

Things to weigh

Important tradeoffs

SST is still relatively new compared to older engineering colleges, and the fees are higher than many traditional options. Those are valid concerns and should be part of the decision.

03.

Who should consider SST?

SST may make sense if you:

  • ✓ are genuinely interested in coding and software development
  • ✓ enjoy building projects or solving programming problems
  • ✓ want a peer group where many students are focused on tech
  • ✓ are okay with joining a relatively new institution
  • ✓ can comfortably manage the fees

A traditional BTech may be safer if you:

  • → want the security of a long-established college
  • → prefer a broader engineering curriculum
  • → care a lot about lower overall cost
  • → want a larger and more conventional campus experience
04.

NSET Exam Guide

What is in NSET?

  • Mathematics
  • Logical Reasoning

The maths section can be fairly difficult, so it should not be ignored. Logical reasoning is also important because it helps maximize the overall score.

How to think about preparation

  • Practice mathematics consistently
  • Work on logical reasoning speed and accuracy
  • Remember that there is also an individual cutoff for mathematics
  • Be ready for interview rounds after the written exam
After the written test, candidates usually go through multiple interview rounds. A common pattern is 2 to 3 interviews, depending on performance and process flow.
