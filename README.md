# My Experience at Scaler School of Technology

I’m currently in my first year at SST in Bengaluru.

From my experience so far, the environment is quite focused on coding, development, projects, and problem solving. A lot of students here actually spend time building things, practicing programming, or improving their development skills, which makes for a positive peer environment.

Personally, one of the things I liked is that the curriculum starts early with subjects that feel much closer to software development rather than spending a long time on unrelated engineering coursework first.

The campus itself is relatively small compared to large traditional universities, but the surrounding area is calm and green.

The hostel is around **1.5 km from campus**, so commuting is manageable. Students also get **Cult gym passes**, and there are multiple gyms nearby.

Hostel room options include:

- double
- triple
- double-large

The rooms are not extremely spacious, but they are structured well enough to be comfortable, and they have windows.

For food, there are multiple vendors and students can choose between them or opt out depending on preference.

# NSET Exam Guide

To get into SST, you need to clear the **NSET (National Scaler Entrance Test)**.

## What is in NSET?
From my experience, the exam includes:

- **Mathematics**
- **Logical Reasoning**

## How difficult is NSET?
The **math section can be fairly difficult**, but you should not ignore it as there are subject wise cutoff.

At the same time, **logical reasoning is important for maximizing your overall score**.

A practical way to think about it is:

- do well in LR to strengthen your overall performance
- but do not neglect maths, because there is also an **individual cutoff for mathematics**

## Are there interviews after NSET?
Yes.

After the written test, candidates usually go through interview rounds.

I personally had **two interviews**, while some students had **three**, depending on performance and process flow.
